About

I am a Research Fellow at the Department of Data Science and AI, Monash University. My research focuses on Time Series Analysis and Machine Learning applications. I completed my PhD at the Faculty of Information Technology, Monash University, under the supervision of Professor Geoff Webb, Dr François Petitjean and Dr Paul Reichl. My thesis titled “Time series classification at scale” focused on Scalable Time Series Classification, where I developed faster and more efficient algorithms to handle large time series datasets. I am fortunate to be awarded the Mollie Holman medal by Monash University for the best doctoral thesis in the Faculty of Information Technology. I have also applied time series analysis to improve railway track maintenance in collaboration with the Institute of Railway Technology (IRT) at Monash University.


Currently I am working on Time Series Extrinsic Regression (TSER), where the task is to predict a continuous scalar value from a time series. This task differs from Classification where the target is continous, and differs from Forecasting where the prediction does not necessarily depends on recent/seasonal values and the target may not necessarily come from the same series. I am also interested in developing novel methods for machine learning applications using EEG devices such as Epilepsy diagnosis and driver's distraction detection. I believe that both of these research will have significant impact for Epilepsy patients and improving road safety. In addition, I am also providing data science services to Stemly on developing autonomous supply chain demand forecasting solutions.
